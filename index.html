Visualization of the results of N riffle shuffles of a 60 card deck. Each pixel is a normalized representation of how often the card starting at index X (horiz) ends up at index Y (vertical). Full black = most common, full white = least common

Each riffle shuffle is done by perfectly cutting the deck in two, and then repeatedly selecting a random deck to pull one card from. I think this simulates the innaccuracies of a normal shuffle.

The true shuffle is an algorithmic uniform random shuffle of the cards.
